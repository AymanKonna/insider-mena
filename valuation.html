Knowledge Base

A quick reference guide for all the key financial and operational terms used in this report.

  • Total Revenues: The sum of all generated and collected incomes.

  • Revenues - YouTube: The sum of all generated and collected from the YouTube platform.

  • Revenues - DSPs: The sum of all generated and collected from Digital Streaming Platforms (DSPs), which are services like Spotify, Apple Music, etc., excluding YouTube.

  • Revenues - Content Licensing: The sum of all generated and collected from licensing services, where the company's content is licensed for use by others.

  • Revenues - Facebook Ent.: The sum of all generated and collected from the Facebook Entertainment Platform.

  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): The direct costs attributable to the production of the goods or services sold by a company. In this case, it's the sum of all content costs and shares paid to rights holders.

  • Gross Profit: The company's financial gain after deducting the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) from Total Revenues.

  • Operating Expenses (OpEx): The ongoing costs of running a business's day-to-day operations, such as rent, salaries, utilities, and marketing expenses.

  • Payroll: The sum of annual amounts paid to a company's staff.

  • Tech/Platform Maintenance: The expenses for updating and enhancing the company's delivery platform.

  • Rent: The annual expense for renting business properties or spaces.

  • General & Admin (G&A): The sum of all necessary costs to maintain a company's daily operations and administer its business, not including expenses directly related to a product or service.

  • Net Income: The final company profit after deducting all operating and non-operating expenses, as well as taxes, from total revenue.

  • DSPs (Digital Streaming Platforms): The streaming platforms that distribute content, such as Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music.

  • EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization): A measure of a company's overall financial performance and is used as an alternative to simple earnings or net income in some circumstances.
