Major Labels and Streaming Giants Vie for Dominance in a Rapidly Growing Market

Market Overview

The Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region has emerged as the world's fastest-growing music market, experiencing a meteoric rise driven by a young, digitally savvy population and the proliferation of streaming services. Since 2019, major international music labels and Digital Service Providers (DSPs) have significantly expanded their operations, vying for a strategic foothold in this burgeoning landscape. This report assesses the progress of these players, shaped by a complex interplay of localization, strategic partnerships, and adaptation to unique market dynamics.

Major Labels: Deepening Local Roots

Universal Music Group (UMG)

UMG has been particularly bullish on the region, establishing a regional headquarters in Dubai and dedicated offices in key markets. Their strategy focuses on signing and developing local and regional artists, recognizing the immense popularity of Arabic music. UMG actively integrates its international catalog with a growing roster of local talent, fostering collaborations and cross-promotional opportunities.

Sony Music Entertainment

Sony has intensified its focus on the MENA region through strategic partnerships and signings. The label has been active in acquiring and partnering with prominent local music companies and artists. This approach allows them to leverage existing local expertise and artist rosters while providing the global reach and resources of a major label to tap into the region's diverse musical landscape.

Warner Music Group (WMG)

WMG has made significant strides by acquiring regional distributor Qanawat Music and investing in talent agency HuManagement. These moves underscore a clear intent to deepen its roots in the local ecosystem, providing WMG with enhanced distribution capabilities and direct access to a pipeline of emerging and established artists.

Digital Service Providers: The Streaming Battleground

Spotify

A dominant force, Spotify has invested heavily in localization, curating playlists for local tastes and launching initiatives to support emerging artists. Its brand recognition and user-friendly interface have driven widespread adoption.

Anghami

The leading homegrown platform, Anghami, remains a formidable competitor with a deep understanding of the regional music scene and a loyal user base, leveraging its early entry and focus on Arabic content.

Apple Music

Benefiting from its integration into the Apple ecosystem, Apple Music maintains a strong presence. It has made significant efforts to curate local content and playlists to cater to regional preferences.

Deezer

Deezer has been active in the region by securing partnerships with local telecom operators to expand its reach, providing a viable path for growth even if it doesn't have the same market penetration as its rivals.

Digital streaming will continue to be the dominant force, building on its current 95.5% share of total recorded music revenue. The next five years will see a sustained surge in paid subscriptions, with growth projected at a healthy 15% annually on a global scale. This is fueled by rising smartphone penetration and a young, digitally native population in the MENA region. The expansion of local-language repertoire and discovery of new talent by platforms will create a self-sustaining cycle of content creation and consumption.

Recorded Music: Beyond Borders

The recorded music sector will thrive as MENA-originated content gains international traction. Indicators like Spotify's 40% annual increase in streams of MENA music outside the region since 2018 show a growing global appetite for this content. The strategic focus on strengthening music exports, supported by industry-wide cooperation and technological integration, will be a key driver for significant revenue increases and will establish the region's artists on the world stage.

Live Performance: A New Era of Experience

Live performances are set for a major renaissance, evolving beyond traditional concerts. Significant government and private sector investment, exemplified by Saudi Arabia's allocation of over $64 billion to music and entertainment, is creating world-class entertainment hubs. The future of live music in the region will be defined by the integration of emerging technologies like AI and VR, creating immersive and interactive fan experiences that draw global audiences and drive new revenue streams.
